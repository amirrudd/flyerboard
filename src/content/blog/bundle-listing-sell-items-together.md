# Sell It as a Set: Bundle Your Items and Cash In Faster

**Some things sell faster together than apart. Bundle Listing lets you group 2 to 4 of your own FlyerBoard ads into one discounted package — one listing, one buyer, one pickup — instead of running three separate sales and fielding three separate haggles. FlyerBoard even does the maths for you, showing buyers exactly how much they're saving.**

You know the drag of selling a matching set piece by piece: one buyer wants the desk but not the chair, another wants the lamp for half what it's worth, and you end up with the leftover nobody wants sitting in the garage for a month. Bundling skips all of that.

## Why does bundling sell faster than listing separately?

Because it removes the friction that kills slow sales.

- **One transaction beats three.** Instead of three buyers, three negotiations and three pickup times, it's one of each.
- **The discount does the selling for you.** A visible "save $45 (18%)" line is a concrete reason to buy the whole set right now, instead of shopping around item by item.
- **It clears the awkward leftovers.** The one piece that never sells alone often moves easily when it's part of a set someone actually wants.
- **Buyers see more value per message.** One inquiry gets them three things — that's a stronger pitch than any single ad can make on its own.

## How do you turn your ads into a bundle?

You're not creating new listings — you're grouping ones you've already posted.

1. From your dashboard, pick 2 to 4 of your own active ads to group together.
2. Set a **bundle price** for the lot.
3. FlyerBoard works out the savings automatically — both the dollar amount and the percentage off, compared to buying each item separately.
4. Publish. Your bundle gets its own shareable page buyers can browse and message you from directly.

An ad can only live in one bundle at a time, so there's no risk of accidentally double-selling something.

## What actually makes a good bundle?

Good bundles share a buyer, not necessarily a category:

- **A room's worth of furniture** — desk, chair, shelf.
- **A stage-of-life set** — cot, high chair, baby monitor for a growing family.
- **A hobby starter kit** — tent, sleeping bag, camp stove for someone setting up to camp.
- **A matched electronics set** — monitor, keyboard, mouse.

If someone buying one of these would plausibly want the others too, it's a bundle waiting to happen.

## How do you price it so it actually sells?

- **Add up what each item would fetch alone.** You need that total to show a real saving.
- **Make the discount worth noticing.** 5% off barely registers; 15–25% gives buyers a genuine reason to take the whole set instead of shopping around.
- **Round the number.** $120 reads better and closes faster than $118.40.
- **Don't undersell your best piece.** If one item in the set is worth far more than the rest, price the bundle so it still reflects that.

## Why not just cram everything into one Facebook Marketplace listing?

Because that's the workaround people already use, and it's a bad one. You've seen the listing: a single photo collage of five unrelated things, one vague price, and a caption saying "will separate." Buyers can't see individual condition or details, can't save just the one item they actually want, and every message starts with "is this still available / which parts are you keeping."

A FlyerBoard bundle isn't a photo collage — it's a proper listing with each item's own photos and details still visible, one clear combined price, and the savings spelled out so there's no guessing what a fair offer looks like. It's the difference between "here's a pile of stuff, make an offer" and an actual product a buyer can say yes to on the spot.

If it's a handful of related items, bundle them. If it's an entire house, [Moving Sale Mode](/blog/moving-sale-mode-list-everything-at-once) is built for that scale instead.

## Where does a bundle actually show up?

Right in the normal FlyerBoard feed, same as any other listing — buyers don't need to know a special "bundle mode" exists to stumble onto yours. On the ad card, it shows a strip of thumbnails from every item inside instead of a single photo, so people can tell at a glance it's a set, not one item. Clicking through takes them to the bundle's own page with the full lineup, the combined photos, and the price with savings spelled out. They can save it to come back to later, exactly like they'd save any single ad, and message you about the whole set from one thread instead of three.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Can I bundle items that are already part of a Moving Sale?**
No — an ad can only belong to one grouping at a time, either a standalone bundle or a Moving Sale.

**Q: What if a buyer only wants some of the bundled items?**
Bundles sell as a set at the bundle price, but nothing stops you negotiating in the message thread — you can always un-bundle and sell items individually if that works better.

**Q: How many items can go in a bundle?**
Between 2 and 4.

**Q: Do I have to offer a discount?**
The price is up to you, but buyers respond best when there's a real, visible saving next to it.
