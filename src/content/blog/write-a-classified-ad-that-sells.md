# How to Write a Classified Ad That Sells Fast: 9 Tips

**To sell fast, write a clear keyword-rich title, list honest details (brand, size, age, faults), add several well-lit photos that show the item from real angles, and set a fair price based on what similar items are going for. Then reply quickly, make pickup easy, and keep the conversation on FlyerBoard so the whole thing stays simple and safe.** The nine tips below walk through each step.

## Why does a good listing sell faster?

A buyer decides in seconds. They scan your photo, read your title, glance at the price, and either tap through or scroll on. A clear, honest, well-priced ad removes the friction and the doubt — so the right buyer says yes before someone else's listing catches their eye.

You don't need to be a copywriter. You just need to answer the questions a buyer would ask, before they have to ask them.

## The 9 tips

### 1. Write a clear, keyword-rich title

Your title is the headline that decides whether anyone clicks. Say exactly what the item is, the way a buyer would search for it.

- Include the brand, model, and one key detail: *"IKEA Malm Double Bed Frame — White, Excellent Condition"* beats *"Bed for sale"*.
- Put the most important words first; many people skim the start of a title.
- Skip ALL CAPS, rows of emojis, and words like "URGENT" — they read as noise, not value.

### 2. Be honest about condition and key details

Trust sells. The fastest sales happen when the buyer already knows what they're getting and turns up ready to pay.

Include the details people always end up asking about:

- **Brand and model** — so buyers can look it up.
- **Size or dimensions** — width, height, depth in cm; clothing and shoe sizes.
- **Age and condition** — "two years old, used daily" is fine; honesty builds confidence.
- **Faults and wear** — a scratch, a stain, a missing remote. Naming flaws up front saves a wasted pickup and a bad review.
- **What's included** — cables, original box, spare parts, manuals.

### 3. Take good photos

Photos do most of the selling. A clear set of real photos beats a clever description every time.

- Shoot in **natural light** — near a window, not under harsh ceiling lights or with flash.
- Use a **clean, plain background** so the item is the star.
- Show **multiple angles**: front, back, sides, and any important detail.
- **Show the flaws** too — a close-up of the scratch reassures buyers more than hiding it.
- Use your own photos, never stock images. Buyers want to see the actual item.

### 4. Price it fairly with a quick bit of research

Right-pricing is the single biggest lever on how fast something sells. Before you post, search FlyerBoard for the same or similar items and see what they're listed at.

| Pricing approach | Likely result |
|---|---|
| Priced above the market | Sits unsold; few messages |
| Priced at the fair market rate | Steady interest, sells in good time |
| Priced slightly below market | Sells fast, often within days |

If you want room to negotiate, add a small buffer and mark it "ONO" (or nearest offer). If you want it gone this week, price it sharp and say so. And if it's something hard to put a price on, you could [trade it for what you actually want](/blog/trade-and-swap-items-flyerboard) instead.

### 5. Include what matters, leave out what doesn't

Give buyers everything they need to decide — and nothing that clutters the decision.

**Include:** the item's full story (use, age, why you're selling), measurements, condition, what's included, and your suburb or general pickup area.

**Leave out:** your full address, phone number, email, or other personal contact details. Keep those private and let buyers reach you through in-app chat. It's safer and keeps every message in one place.

### 6. Hook them with the first line

The first line of your description is the second thing a buyer reads, after the title. Lead with the strongest reason to buy.

- *"Barely used — bought for a nursery we never finished setting up."*
- *"Selling our reliable winter heater now that we've moved to ducted."*

A short, honest reason builds trust and answers the unspoken question: *why are you selling this?*

### 7. Respond fast

Speed wins sales. Buyers often message several sellers at once, and the first to reply usually gets the deal. Turn on notifications so you see new messages quickly, and answer with the details and a suggested pickup time. A reply within a few hours keeps a keen buyer warm; a reply two days later usually finds them gone.

### 8. Make pickup and location clear

Sort out the logistics in the listing so there's nothing to negotiate later.

- State your **general area** (suburb or region), not your exact address.
- Say whether it's **pickup only**, or if you can deliver locally and at what cost.
- Mention rough **availability** — "evenings and weekends" — so buyers know it's workable.
- For big items, note if they'll need **help lifting** or a ute or trailer.

### 9. Close safely

A safe sale is a smooth sale. Keep your guard up and the handover stays easy for everyone.

- **Keep chat on FlyerBoard.** If someone pushes to move to text, email, or another app early, that's a red flag — staying in-app keeps a record and protects you.
- **Meet in a public place** in daylight for smaller items; a busy car park or café works well.
- For larger pickups at home, **have someone with you** and keep the exchange at the door or driveway.
- **Be wary of [overpayment, courier, or "I'll pay extra" scams](/blog/avoid-marketplace-scams-australia).** For local sales, cash on pickup is simplest. If you're unsure, [Scamwatch](https://www.scamwatch.gov.au/) lists the common ones.

## A quick pre-post checklist

Before you hit publish, run through this:

- Title names the item, brand, and a key detail.
- Description covers condition, size, age, faults, and what's included.
- At least three clear, well-lit photos from different angles.
- Price checked against similar FlyerBoard listings.
- Pickup area and availability stated.
- No personal contact details in the listing.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How many photos should a classified ad have?**
At least three, ideally four to six — a main photo plus different angles and any flaws. More clear photos mean fewer questions and faster sales.

**Q: Should I put my price or write "make an offer"?**
Always include a price. "Make an offer" makes buyers guess and slows things down. Add "ONO" if you're open to negotiating.

**Q: Why isn't my item selling?**
Usually price or photos. Compare your price to similar listings, swap in brighter photos, and tighten the title. Bumping or refreshing the listing helps too.

**Q: Is it safe to share my phone number in a listing?**
Better not to. Use FlyerBoard's in-app chat to keep your contact details private and every message in one place until you've agreed on a sale.
