# How to Run a Successful Moving Sale: A Step-by-Step Checklist

**A successful moving sale comes down to starting early, sorting everything into keep, sell, donate and bin, then pricing realistically and listing it all in one place.** Give yourself two to three weeks, photograph items in good light, price with round numbers and bundle deals, and promote locally. ## When should you start planning a moving sale?

Start two to three weeks before you want buyers turning up. Decluttering always takes longer than you think, and a rushed sale means underpricing good gear just to get rid of it.

Work backwards from your moving date. Block out one weekend to sort, a few evenings to photograph and list, and a clear day (or two) for pickups. The earlier your listings go live, the more people see them and the more you sell before the truck arrives.

## How do you sort everything before a moving sale?

Touch each item once and put it in a pile. The fastest method is the four-pile sort:

- **Keep** — things going with you to the new place.
- **Sell** — anything in working, sellable condition someone would pay for.
- **Donate** — usable items that won't sell or aren't worth the effort.
- **Bin/recycle** — broken, stained or unsafe items. Be honest here.

Here's a quick guide for the grey-area items:

| Item type | Usually sell | Usually donate | Bin / recycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Furniture | Solid, clean, working | Dated but usable | Broken frames, mould |
| Electronics | Working, with cables | Older but functional | Dead, no charger |
| Clothing | Near-new, brand items | Clean everyday wear | Stained, torn |
| Kitchenware | Full sets, appliances | Odd mugs, spare cutlery | Cracked, chipped |

Donating keeps usable things out of landfill and supports your local op shop — a small win for the community and the planet on a day that's already about clearing out.

## How should you price items for a moving sale?

Price to sell, not to win. You're clearing a house, not running a shop, so be realistic about secondhand value.

A few rules that keep things moving:

- **Use round numbers.** $5, $10, $20, $50 — easy to read and easy to pay in cash.
- **Aim for a fraction of retail.** Most used goods sell at 20–40% of what they cost new, depending on condition.
- **Bundle the small stuff.** "All books $2 each or 6 for $10" shifts volume fast.
- **Leave a little wiggle room.** Buyers love a small haggle, so price a touch above your real floor.
- **Mark the must-gos.** Anything you absolutely won't move should be cheap or free by the last day.

## How do you photograph and list everything well?

Good photos do most of the selling before anyone messages you. Shoot in daylight near a window, wipe items down first, and use a plain background so the item stands out.

Then write a short, honest description for each: what it is, rough dimensions or size, condition, and any flaws. Naming flaws upfront saves you time-wasting messages and builds trust.

## How do you promote a moving sale locally?

Go where your neighbours already are. The most effective promotion is hyper-local:

- Post your sale link in local community and "buy nothing" or marketplace groups for your suburb.
- Message friends and neighbours directly — word of mouth moves the big items.
- Mention it to anyone nearby who's also moving or setting up a home.
- If you're doing an in-person pickup day, a clear sign on your street helps passers-by.

Keep the message simple: what you're selling, the suburb, and the one link.

## When is the best time to hold a moving sale?

Weekend mornings win. Saturday and Sunday from around 8am catch early browsers and people with time to collect bulky items before the day fills up.

List as early as you can, though — many items sell during the week to people who can't wait for the weekend. Avoid clashing with long weekends or big public holidays when everyone's away.

## How do you handle pickups safely?

Keep it simple and sensible. A few habits make pickup day smooth:

- **Meet in a safe spot.** A driveway, front porch or busy public place is better than inviting strangers deep inside your home.
- **Have someone with you** for larger items or evening pickups.
- **Take cash or instant transfer**, and confirm the payment has landed before the item leaves.
- **Set clear pickup windows** so you're not on call all day.

For more on staying safe, read our guide to [spotting and avoiding marketplace scams](/blog/avoid-marketplace-scams-australia) — and [Scamwatch](https://www.scamwatch.gov.au/) has practical Australian guidance too.

## What do you do with leftovers?

Don't let leftovers move with you. On the last day, drop prices hard, offer "free to a good home" on the dregs, or list a "moving — everything must go" bundle.

Whatever's left after that, take straight to an op shop or recycling. The goal is an empty house, not a second sale next month.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How long does a moving sale take to organise?**
A: Plan for two to three weeks. Sorting and listing take the most time; the actual selling can happen over a few days once items are live.

**Q: What sells best at a moving sale?**
A: Furniture, working appliances, kitchenware, tools and near-new clothing tend to move fastest. Clean, clearly photographed items with honest descriptions always do better.

**Q: Should I price items individually or in bundles?**
A: Both. Price big items individually with round numbers, and bundle low-value small items ("6 for $10") to clear volume quickly.

**Q: Is it safe to have strangers come to my house?**
A: Meet at your driveway or a public spot where possible, have someone with you, and confirm payment before the item leaves. See [Scamwatch](https://www.scamwatch.gov.au/) for more.
