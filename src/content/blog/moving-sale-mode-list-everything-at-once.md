# Moving House? Clear the Whole Place in One Sale, Free

**Packing up a house means dozens of things to sell, and nobody has time to write forty separate ads. Moving Sale Mode lets you bulk-photograph, bulk-price and publish the entire lot as one sale with a single shareable link — free, with no limit on how many items you list.**

You know the moment: moving day is booked, and half the house still needs a buyer. Posting item by item at that point isn't realistic. Moving Sale Mode is built for exactly this — get it all in front of buyers at once, and get on with packing.

## Why is bulk-listing the whole house better than posting one by one?

Because at moving-house volume, one-ad-at-a-time is the thing that stops people from listing at all.

- **One flow instead of forty.** Photograph and price your items in a single batch pass, not a fresh form each time.
- **One link to share, not a wall of ads.** Send neighbours, group chats and Facebook groups a single sale page instead of a list of separate URLs.
- **One card in the feed, not a flood.** Your whole sale shows up as one entry in the FlyerBoard feed with a 2×2 thumbnail preview — you're not spamming the feed with forty listings.
- **Nothing gets left behind.** Stuff that would never get its own ad — a box of kitchenware, a stack of kids' books — still finds a buyer as part of the sale.

## How does it actually work?

It's five steps, and none of them involve filling out the same ad form forty times:

1. **Name your sale.** Give it a title — "Bondi apartment clear-out," "everything must go before the 30th."
2. **Dump in all your photos at once.** Snap every item and upload the whole lot in one go, instead of starting fresh for each thing.
3. **Run down the list and price as you go.** Give each item a title, price and condition in a quick pass — like ticking boxes, not writing essays.
4. **Tie a few things together if it helps.** Group items that belong as a set right there while you're pricing.
5. **Hit publish.** Your sale goes live on its own page and lands in the main feed for buyers to find.

List as much as you want — there's no cap on how many things you can put in a single sale.

## Is it really free?

Yes — properly free, not free-until-you-hit-publish. Every step that gets your sale live and in front of buyers costs nothing: setting it up, uploading, pricing, grouping, publishing. If you later want to pay to give the sale an extra push, that option's there — but you never have to, and the whole sale works without it.

## What should actually make the cut?

Don't be precious about listing every single thing individually:

- **Group small stuff together** — "box of kitchenware", "kids' books, 20+" — rather than one ad per mug.
- **Give big furniture its own price and photos** even inside the sale, since it deserves the attention.
- **Bundle things that go together** — a desk and chair, a cot and change table — using the built-in bundling step.
- **Skip anything not worth the photo.** Genuinely low-value junk is better donated than listed.

## When should you actually list — and how long does it take?

Do it as you pack, not the night before the move. Photographing and roughly pricing items in short batches over a few evenings, room by room, beats trying to shoot the whole house in one sitting. Most of the effort is deciding what's worth listing, not the listing mechanics — and whatever you haven't gotten to by moving day, you can still add later since the sale stays open until you close it.

## Why not just do what everyone else does — a Facebook post or a garage sale sign?

Because both fall apart at moving-house volume. A single Facebook post with forty photos jammed into one carousel gives buyers no way to ask about just the desk without scrolling through kids' clothes and kitchenware first — every "is this still available" comment applies to nothing in particular. A garage sale sign only reaches people who happen to drive past that weekend, and you're standing outside all day instead of packing.

Moving Sale Mode gives every item its own entry inside one sale, so a buyer can click straight to the desk, see just its photos and price, and message you about that one thing — while still browsing everything else you're clearing out. And because the sale lives on FlyerBoard instead of a driveway, it keeps working while you're inside packing boxes.

If it's only a handful of related items rather than a whole house, a [Bundle Listing](/blog/bundle-listing-sell-items-together) is the more focused version of the same idea.

The sale itself appears as one card in the main feed; buyers click through to your sale page to browse everything inside it.

Yes — the review step lets you group a few items into a bundle before you publish.
