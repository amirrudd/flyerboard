# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Everyday local buyers and sellers, mostly on their phones, in short quick sessions. A seller is usually clearing out, moving, or offloading a few things and wants the listing up and in front of nearby buyers with the least possible fuss. A buyer is scanning the feed for something specific or just browsing for a deal, and wants to reach the seller without friction. The same person is often both. Sessions are short and interruptible — the design has to work one-handed and pick up where it left off.

## Product Purpose

FlyerBoard is a classified marketplace for local buying and selling ("flyers" = ads). It exists to make posting and finding local listings fast, clean, and trustworthy — closing the gap between "I have a thing to sell" and "someone messaged me about it," and between "I want this" and "I'm talking to the seller." Success looks like a listing posted in seconds, a feed worth scrolling, and a conversation started without the visitor ever hitting clutter, a scam smell, or a dead end.

## Positioning

A cleaner, friendlier classifieds — the buy-and-sell experience of Gumtree or Marketplace without the clutter, the scams, or the friction. Every screen should feel calmer and more considered than the incumbents it's replacing.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, trustworthy, and effortless, with a modern, clean, confident finish. The voice is that of a helpful neighbour, not a corporation — plain, warm, human, never salesy. Confident enough to feel premium and safe to transact on, but never cold or aloof. Copy is direct and reassuring; the interface gets out of the way of the transaction.

## Anti-references

Not the cluttered, spammy feel of old Gumtree — no dense grey listing walls, ad-choked pages, dated chrome, or scam energy. Not a cold corporate SaaS dashboard — no enterprise-navy sterility or data-tool coldness stripped of warmth. Not a loud discount-retail marketplace — no screaming SALE badges, coupon-site chaos, or aggressive upsell on every surface. Warmth and trust come first; loudness and clutter are the failure modes.

## Design Principles

Cleaner than the incumbents. Every screen should earn its density and feel calmer than the classifieds sites it replaces; when in doubt, remove.

Trust is the product. Safe-to-transact signals, honest states, and clear ownership beat hype — people are meeting strangers to exchange money and goods, and the interface has to feel safe enough to do that.

Effortless list-to-message. Minimize the steps between having a thing and having a listing, and between seeing a listing and reaching the seller. Friction anywhere on that path is a bug.

Feed-first, newest on top. The feed is the product's heartbeat; it stays chronological and honest (pinning/boosting is the model, not sort controls). Don't bury it under chrome.

Warm, not loud. Personality is carried by tone, spacing, and restraint — not by shouting. Friendly beats aggressive; confident beats busy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Not a formal focus for this doc yet. In practice the app is mobile-first and one-handed by default, and motion already respects `prefers-reduced-motion` through the shared `useMotionPrefs` helper — keep both as you build. A formal WCAG target can be added here when it becomes a priority.
